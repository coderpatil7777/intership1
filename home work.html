Here are the top 5 businessmen in India as of 2025,

Mukesh Ambani

Mukesh Ambani is one of India's most influential and successful businessmen. Born on April 19, 1957, in Aden (now Yemen), he is the chairman and managing director of Reliance Industries Limited (RIL), a Fortune 500 company and Indias most valuable firm by market capitalization. Mukesh is the son of the legendary industrialist Dhirubhai Ambani, who founded Reliance. Under Mukesh Ambanis leadership, Reliance expanded from petrochemicals and refining into sectors like telecom, retail, and digital services. His telecom venture Reliance Jio, launched in 2016, transformed Indias digital landscape by providing affordable internet and mobile services to millions. Mukesh Ambani is also a major player in Indias green energy transition, investing heavily in solar, hydrogen, and other sustainable technologies. He is among the richest people in the world, with a net worth of over $100 billion. Apart from business, he is known for his luxurious lifestyle and lives in Antilia, one of the worlds most expensive private homes in Mumbai. Despite his wealth, he is deeply involved in philanthropic activities through the Reliance Foundation, led by his wife, Nita Ambani. His visionary leadership continues to shape Indias economic future.

Gautam Adani

Gautam Adani is a prominent Indian industrialist and the founder and chairman of the Adani Group, a multinational conglomerate based in India. Born on June 24, 1962, in Ahmedabad, Gujarat, Adani started his career as a diamond sorter and later moved into commodity trading, eventually establishing the Adani Group in 1988. Under his leadership, the group has expanded rapidly into various sectors including infrastructure, ports, power generation, renewable energy, mining, natural gas, and airports. One of his most significant achievements is the development of Mundra Port, Indias largest commercial port. In recent years, Adani has become a key figure in Indias push toward renewable energy, with ambitious plans to become the worlds largest producer of green energy. Despite facing financial and political controversies, he remains one of the wealthiest individuals in India and the world, with a net worth often crossing $80 billion. Gautam Adani is known for his strategic vision, aggressive business expansion, and strong influence in Indias infrastructure development. His rise from a small businessman to a global industrial powerhouse is often cited as a modern Indian success story.

Shiv Nadar

Shiv Nadar is a renowned Indian industrialist, philanthropist, and the founder of HCL Technologies, one of India’s leading global IT services companies. Born on July 14, 1945, in Tamil Nadu, he began his career in the 1960s as an engineer before venturing into entrepreneurship. In 1976, he founded HCL (Hindustan Computers Limited) with the vision of building an Indian computer company at a time when the IT industry was still in its infancy. Under his leadership, HCL grew from a small startup into a global IT powerhouse, offering software services, IT infrastructure, and digital solutions across the world. Apart from his business success, Shiv Nadar is widely respected for his commitment to education and philanthropy. He established the Shiv Nadar Foundation, which runs prestigious institutions like Shiv Nadar University and VidyaGyan schools for underprivileged students. Over the years, he has donated billions to educational causes, making him one of India’s most generous philanthropists. With a net worth of around $30 billion, Shiv Nadar continues to play a key role in shaping India’s knowledge economy, combining business excellence with a strong sense of social responsibility.

Radhakishan Damani

Radhakishan Damani is a respected Indian billionaire investor, entrepreneur, and the founder of DMart (Avenue Supermarts), one of India’s most successful retail chains. Born in 1945 in Mumbai, Maharashtra, he started his career as a stock market investor and trader, where he earned a reputation for being a calm and disciplined value investor. Known for his simple lifestyle and sharp business acumen, Damani was a mentor to many, including fellow billionaire Rakesh Jhunjhunwala. In 2002, he entered the retail business by launching the first DMart store in Mumbai. His business strategy focused on offering quality products at low prices, mainly in middle-class neighborhoods. This approach made DMart highly successful, growing into a nationwide chain with hundreds of stores. The company’s IPO in 2017 was a massive success, and it became one of the most profitable retail businesses in India. Despite being one of the richest people in India, with a net worth of around $25 billion, Radhakishan Damani maintains a low profile, avoids media attention, and is known for hi s humble and private nature. His journey from stock market investing to retail giant reflects a deep understanding of both finance and consumer behavior in India.
